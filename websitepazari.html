Gjirokastër Old Bazaar

About the Old Bazaar

The Old Bazaar of Gjirokastër is a historical marketplace dating back to the Ottoman era. Located in the heart of the city, it has been a center of trade, culture, and craftsmanship for centuries.

History

Originally built in the 17th century, the bazaar has undergone various transformations due to fires and reconstructions. Despite these changes, it retains its traditional charm, with cobblestone streets and characteristic stone-roofed buildings.

Main Attractions

  • Handcrafted souvenirs and traditional Albanian goods.
  • Local artisans showcasing woodwork, pottery, and textiles.
  • Traditional cafes and restaurants serving authentic cuisine.
  • Beautiful Ottoman-era architecture and historic buildings.
  • A lively marketplace atmosphere with local vendors and street performances.

Interesting Facts

The Old Bazaar is famous for its well-preserved Ottoman design and its role in connecting merchants from different regions. Many of the shops are family-run businesses passed down for generations.

It is also a major tourist attraction, offering visitors a glimpse into Albania's rich heritage and local craftsmanship.

Visiting Information

The bazaar is open daily, with the best time to visit being in the morning or late afternoon. Guided tours are available, and it is a must-see destination for anyone exploring Gjirokastër.
